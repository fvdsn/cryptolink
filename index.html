What's CryptoLink ?

CryptoLink is a open source web application that allows you to easily encrypt and share text messages, websites and pictures with a high level of security and privacy.

More specifically, CryptoLink encrypts the data into a link (or URL). All you have to do to decrypt your data is to follow the link and provide your password. There is nothing to install, and it works on almost every device.

Cryptolink doesn't know anything about your message. It doesn't host it, it doesn't know you encrypted it and neither does it know when you decrypt it. This means we can let you do whatever you want with CryptoLink and you can rest assured that as long as you have the link, you have your data.

How secure are cryptolinks?

First, keep in mind that CryptoLink is beta software with all the usual warnings it implies.

Cryptolinks are encrypted with AES, one of the most secure encryption algorithm available. But good encryption does not protect against easily guessable passwords; with the help of a supercomputer, a hacker can guess millions of passwords a second. It is therefore important for you to choose a good password. Following CryptoLink's password security advice ensures that your data is safe for the next century.

How much data can I put in a cryptolink?

Most browsers allow you to put several megabytes of data inside a link and that is enough for a lot of applications. And by most browsers I mean every browser but Internet Explorer who chokes after a bit more than two thousand characters.

QR-Codes can encode up to ~2900 characters (more than IE!) but at those sizes they are not really that practical.
