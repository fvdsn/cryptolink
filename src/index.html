What are Cryptolinks ?

In a word a cryptolink is an URL or 'link' that contains encrypted data.

TODO: picture

Previously, if you wanted to encrypt and send an message, picture or program, you had to find and install an encryption software on your device and ask all your recipients to do the same. And you may also had to find a service to host the encrypted file, which means you had to depend on the host to accept your file and not remove it later on.

Cryptolink solves all these problems. First, cryptolinks can be encrypted and decrypted in a web browser, which means they work natively with no setup on almost every device. And since the data is encrypted into the cryptolink, you just need to send an URL to your recipients; no hosting service is required. If your recipient can see the link, he can use it.

And as sharing URLs is at the very basis of the web, there are countless practical ways to share your encrypted messages: email, IM, social networks, forums, QR-Codes, etc.

How secure are cryptolinks?

Cryptolinks are encrypted with AES, one of the most secure encryption algorithm available. But good encryption does not protect against easily guessable passwords. With the help of a computer, a hacker can guess millions of passwords a second. It is therefore very important for your chosen password to be hard to guess by following these tips:

  • Follow the indications given by the password input box: If it says it's useless or insecure, then pick another one !
  • Make sure it is long enough, at least 15 characters.
  • Mixing uppercase and lowercase characters, using numbers and special characters also improves the security, especially if you don't follow common patterns
  • Common names and words are easy to remember but also easy to guess, and a long word is as easy to guess as a short one, so it is far better to use many short words than one long. A combination of four words and more should be secure
  • But be aware that even long sentences taken from books, websites you visit, or common english are also very easy to guess
  • Don't use keyboard patterns
  • Don't use a password you use on other services

What if cryptolink.org is taken down ?

                    
Since cryptolink.org doesn't host, view, log or access any encrypted data or user information and since
                    it has no database or dynamic content and is hosted on a very reliable service, 
                    there is little reason for it to ever break or be taken down.
                    

                    But in the case it happens, there are several ways to go around that. First cryptolink is free software,
                    which means you can put up mirrors and change the host in the cryptolink to point to another location. I am also
                    in the process of developping an installable applications that will allow you to create and decrypt cryptolinks
                    without access to this website or any internet connection at all.
