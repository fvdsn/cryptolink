What are Cryptolinks ?

In a sentence, a cryptolink is an URL or 'link' that contains encrypted data.

Previously, if you wanted to encrypt and send an message, picture or program, had to find and install an encryption software on your device, and ask all your recipients to do the same. And you may also had to find a service to host the encrypted file, which means you had to depend on the host to accept your file and not remove it later on.

Cryptolink solves all these problems by allowing you to encrypt and decrypt the messages in a web browser, which means it works natively with no setup on almost every device. And since the data is encrypted into an URL, you just need to send an URL to your recipients; no hosting service is required.

And as sharing URLs is at the very basis of the web, there are countless practical ways to share your encrypted messages: email, IM, social networks, forums, QR-Codes, etc.

How secure are they?
